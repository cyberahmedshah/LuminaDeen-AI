How It Works

Calculating Zakat is straightforward with our step-by-step approach.

01
📋

List Your Assets

Enter all your assets — cash, gold, silver, investments, and business assets that you have owned for one full lunar year (Hawl).

02
⚖️

Check Nisab

We compare your total zakatable wealth against the current Nisab threshold (equivalent to 87.48g of gold or 612.36g of silver).

03
🧮

Calculate 2.5%

If your wealth exceeds Nisab, Zakat is calculated at 2.5% of your total net zakatable assets after deducting liabilities.

04
💚

Pay & Purify

Distribute your Zakat to the 8 eligible categories of recipients as defined in the Quran (Surah At-Tawbah, 9:60).

🥇

Gold Nisab

87.48 grams

The gold Nisab is equivalent to 87.48 grams of pure gold. This is the most commonly used threshold in contemporary scholarship.

🥈

Silver Nisab

612.36 grams

The silver Nisab is 612.36 grams of pure silver. Due to lower silver prices, this often results in a lower threshold — some scholars recommend using this for wider coverage.

📊

Zakat Rate

2.5%

The standard Zakat rate on wealth is 2.5%, applied to all net zakatable assets that have been held for one full lunar year.
